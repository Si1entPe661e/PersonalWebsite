## Motivation

The project starts from a simple observation: labor mobility changes not only who works where, but also which combinations of ideas become possible inside firms. A local move can alter both productivity and the menu of future knowledge recombinations.

## Empirical focus

The current empirical design follows workers across firms and local labor markets, then links those transitions to subsequent inventive outcomes. I am especially interested in whether mobility matters more in dense local ecosystems where complementary skills are easy to match.

## Expected contribution

The intended contribution is modest but concrete. Rather than treating mobility as a residual labor market phenomenon, the paper treats it as one of the mechanisms through which local knowledge structures evolve.
