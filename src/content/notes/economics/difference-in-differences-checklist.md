## Before touching code

I usually want five things clear before doing anything else:

1. what is the treatment timing structure?
2. what is the comparison group really doing?
3. where could anticipation enter?
4. which units can sort in response?
5. what outcome scale is substantively meaningful?

## Why a checklist helps

Difference-in-differences is too familiar. Familiar methods invite lazy reading. A checklist slows that down and keeps the argument attached to behavior rather than to a default regression template.
