## Reading

I try to read with a pen and a keyboard in parallel. The pen slows me down enough to see the argument. The keyboard captures what should survive the reading.

## Coding

For code, I prefer small scripts, explicit directories, and a short path from question to verification. Tooling matters, but not as much as having a project shape that can be reopened without friction.

## Replication

Replication is one of the best ways to convert admiration into understanding. If a result matters enough, it often deserves at least one honest attempt to rebuild it.

## Writing

I write best when short notes accumulate before the formal document begins. The notebook usually comes first. The polished page comes later.
