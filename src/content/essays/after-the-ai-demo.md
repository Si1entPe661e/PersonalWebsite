## Access is not the scarce part

Once a tool becomes broadly available, the bottleneck shifts. The hard problem is no longer whether one can generate something quickly. It is whether one can tell what is worth keeping.

## In research, that means taste

Taste is not a decorative virtue. It is the ability to distinguish a plausible output from a meaningful one, a clean summary from a misleading one, and a convenient shortcut from a damaging simplification.

## Why I care about this

The better tools become, the more valuable careful judgment becomes. That is as true for writing and coding as it is for academic argument.
