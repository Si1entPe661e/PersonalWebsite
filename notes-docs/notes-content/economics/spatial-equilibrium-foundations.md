# Spatial Equilibrium Foundations

## Core intuition

Spatial equilibrium is often introduced as the idea that utility-adjusted opportunities should not differ too much across locations once people are free to move. That sentence is useful, but too compressed to guide empirical work on its own.

## A better operational summary

For applied work, I find it better to think in terms of linked adjustment margins:

- wages
- housing costs
- amenities
- migration costs

Equilibrium is not a single equality. It is a system in which those margins co-move.

## Why I keep this note

This note exists because many later readings quietly assume the framework without restating it. Having a short reference page makes those assumptions easier to spot.
